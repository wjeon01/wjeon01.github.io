work in progress

General equilibrium effects of private adaptation to climate change [Abstract]

What are general equilibrium effects of climate externalities on utility and production when private adaptation is available to households? Biological and behavioral effects of global mean temperature increase can be different when private adaptation is available. Ignoring avoidance behavior may lead to mischaracterizations of social welfare. When private adaptation is available from energy sector (market), capital and labor will reallocate in a decentralized economy. The reallocation may lead to potential negative feedback, increasing carbon emissions. This paper investigates the optimal tax system to correct for climate externalities with the consideration of potential negative feedback.   (Click here to close)
